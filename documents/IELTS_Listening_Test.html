📺 Context: You will hear a conversation between Jackie Chan and Steve Harvey on a TV talk show. Jackie Chan describes a serious head injury he sustained during a movie stunt, explaining what happened and how it affects him today.

Questions 1–4: Multiple Choice

📋 INSTRUCTIONS: Choose the correct letter A, B, or C.

1.  How did Jackie Chan get the hole in his head?

A  He was punched during a real fight

B  A piece of bone was removed after a stunt accident

C  He was injured during a car crash

A  In Hong Kong

B  In Hollywood

C  In Yugoslavia

3.  What does Jackie Chan say the doctor told him about his condition?

A  He would need regular surgery

B  Part of his brain sometimes pushes out through the hole

A  He is calm and unsurprised

B  He is shocked and visibly disturbed

SECTION 2 — Questions 11–20

Should Zoos Exist? — BBC Ideas Documentary

🐾 Context: You will hear a short documentary exploring whether modern zoos are justified. The speaker considers arguments for and against zoos, including their role in conservation, animal welfare concerns, and public education.

Questions 11–14: Multiple Choice

📋 INSTRUCTIONS: Choose the correct letter A, B, or C.

11. According to the speaker, what is the main original purpose of zoos?

A  Conservation of endangered species

B  Public entertainment and curiosity

C  Scientific research

12. Which of the following does the documentary say is a major criticism of zoos?

A  They cost too much money to run

B  Animals are kept in unnatural, confined environments

C  They attract too many tourists

13. What conservation role do modern zoos claim to play?

A  Releasing animals directly into the wild

B  Breeding endangered species and protecting biodiversity

C  Lobbying governments to protect natural habitats

14. What does the speaker suggest about the future of zoos?

A  They should be abolished immediately

B  They must evolve to focus more on conservation and animal welfare

C  They will naturally become less popular

Questions 15–17: Sentence Completion

📋 INSTRUCTIONS: Complete the sentences below. Write NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS for each answer.

15. Critics argue that keeping animals in cages causes them significant _________________________ distress.

16. Some zoos participate in _________________________ programs that aim to reintroduce animals to their natural habitats.

17. The speaker notes that zoo enclosures today are far more _________________________ than they were in the past.

SECTION 3 — Questions 21–30

Is It OK to Use AI When Studying? — BBC Ideas

🤖 Context: You will hear a documentary discussing whether students should use artificial intelligence tools for studying. The speaker explores different perspectives — from educators, students, and researchers — examining the benefits and dangers of AI-assisted learning.

📋 INSTRUCTIONS: Choose the correct letter A, B, or C.

21. What concern do many teachers have about students using AI?

A  AI tools are too expensive for most students

C  AI gives incorrect information most of the time

22. According to the documentary, what can AI be MOST useful for?

A  Writing essays on behalf of students

B  Explaining difficult concepts and giving feedback

C  Replacing teachers in classrooms

23. What analogy does the speaker use to describe a balanced use of AI?

A  Using a calculator in maths class

B  Hiring a personal tutor

C  Reading a textbook passively

24. What does the documentary suggest students SHOULD do when using AI for studying?

A  Copy AI responses directly into their work

B  Use AI as a starting point and then verify and engage critically

C  Only use AI after completing all their own work first

Questions 25–27: Short Answer

25. What skill does the documentary say AI cannot replace in students? _________________________

26. What type of bias does the speaker warn AI tools may contain? _________________________

27. What does the documentary say schools need to update in response to AI? _________________________

SECTION 4 — Questions 31–40

What Staying Up All Night Does to Your Brain — TED-Ed

🧠 Context: You will hear a TED-Ed lecture by Anna Rothschild about the science of sleep deprivation. The speaker explains what happens to the brain and body when a person stays awake all night, covering topics such as adenosine build-up, cognitive impairment, hallucinations, and long-term health effects.

Questions 31–34: Multiple Choice

📋 INSTRUCTIONS: Choose the correct letter A, B, or C.

31. What chemical builds up in the brain the longer a person stays awake?

A  Dopamine

B  Adenosine

C  Cortisol

32. According to the speaker, what cognitive function is MOST impaired after one sleepless night?

A  Long-term memory storage

B  Decision-making and concentration

C  Language processing

33. What unusual experience may occur after about 24 hours without sleep?

A  Memory loss

B  Hallucinations

C  Loss of speech

34. What does the speaker say coffee does in relation to adenosine?

A  It removes adenosine from the brain permanently

B  It blocks adenosine receptors, masking tiredness temporarily

C  It increases adenosine production

Questions 35–37: Note Completion

📋 INSTRUCTIONS: Complete the notes below. Write NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS AND/OR A NUMBER for each answer.

Effects of Sleep Deprivation:

After 17–19 hours: Performance equivalent to having a blood alcohol level of _______________ (35)

After 24 hours: Risk of experiencing _______________ (36) similar to those of some mental health conditions

Long-term: Increased risk of _______________ (37) and cardiovascular disease

Questions 38–40: True / False / Not Given

📋 INSTRUCTIONS: Write TRUE, FALSE, or NOT GIVEN based on what you hear.

38. The human body can fully compensate for lost sleep by sleeping extra hours the following night.  ___________

39. Young people need more sleep than older adults because their brains are still developing.  ___________

40. Chronic sleep deprivation has been linked to a weakened immune system.  ___________
